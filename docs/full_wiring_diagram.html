Proposed Power Architecture

The final supply, branch fuses, grounding topology, and toolhead regulator remain TBD. Do not power the 6V actuator from the raw stepper supply.

Stepper Motor Wiring

The selected STEPPERONLINE 17HS15-1504S-X1 motors are 4-lead bipolar NEMA 17 steppers rated 1.5A/phase. Wire each motor to its TB6600 output as Black to A+, Green to A-, Red to B+, and Blue to B-.

Driver Current

Set each TB6600 current switch to the closest setting at or below 1.5A/phase for first testing. Increase only after checking motor temperature and missed steps.

A Axis

The A motor drives the rotary bed through a 12:1 GT2 belt reduction. The 14-inch slewing bearing supports the bed load, so the motor mainly provides controlled angular motion.

Bring-Up Order

Power the controller first, verify grblHAL communication, test one driver and motor at low current, then add Y, A, and the pen actuator one subsystem at a time.
