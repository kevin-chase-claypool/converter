## Confirmed identity

The front silkscreen identifies the board as:

```text
RP23CNC  RP23U5XBB V1.01
Copyright © 2024, 25 Brookwood Design
```

This resolves the earlier reported `RP23U5BB` name. The received board is an
`RP23U5XBB V1.01`.

## Visible assembly

The photographs show:

- Three perimeter screw-terminal strips installed.
- USB-C and microSD connectors populated.
- RP2350B controller and supporting surface-mount electronics populated.
- Two six-position Ethernet-module sockets installed at the footprint marked
  `Front WIZ850io`.
- Step/direction headers, digital I/O headers, UART headers, and basic
  configuration headers installed.
- The Ethernet module is not installed in the sockets in these photographs.
- No obvious missing perimeter terminal strip or grossly reversed connector is
  visible.

The back photograph shows solder on the installed through-hole connectors.
Image distance, focus, and angle are not sufficient to certify every joint or
exclude small bridges, cold joints, or opens.

## Configuration details visible

- The board is revision V1.01, which the manual describes as relocating the 5V
  source and digital-input headers relative to V1.0.
- The front-side 5V source selector is present and labeled `SWC USB`.
- The back contains the alternate `5V SRC` solder-jumper footprint.
- Board-side Ethernet sockets match the supplied W5500 module's two six-pin
  rows.

Jumper positions and electrical configuration must be checked directly before
power; the photographs are not a substitute for that inspection.

## Test status

E-16 has substantial evidence: the main board identity/revision and Ethernet
module type are recorded. It remains open until all supplied kit items and any
missing or damaged parts are explicitly recorded.

E-17 remains open. Required evidence:

1. Magnified inspection of every added through-hole joint.
2. Verification that terminal openings and headers have the intended
   orientation.
3. Resistance/continuity checks for shorts between power rails and ground.
4. Verification of 5V source and other jumper positions before power.
